NiiVue Sheet loading

What this demo highlights
  • Multi-instance niivue on a single WebGL2 canvas — 9 independent NiiVue instances share one <canvas>, each with its own normalized bounds rect. Each cell loads a different volume, mesh colormap and orientation.
  • Minimap (top-right) is the primary navigator — click to recentre, drag the blue view rect to pan, scroll the wheel over it to zoom around the cursor. The NVCanvasViewportController reads from the minimap and fans the resulting viewport out to all 9 cells in one pass so the sheet pans/zooms as a single rigid surface.
  • Right-drag on the sheet itself or wheel-zoom over a cell also work — they go through the same controller, so the minimap stays in sync.
  • LOD-driven layout: gutters and per-cell label strips grow with zoom (world-space units) so the layout stays proportional whether the sheet is fit-to-screen or panned in close.
  • Mesh + volume overlay alignment per cell — the .mz3 hemisphere mesh is scaled-and-anchored into each volume's bbox so the AABB stays symmetric.
  • Volumes stream from the IIIF server (/volumes/<id>/raw); meshes come from @niivue/dev-images.
